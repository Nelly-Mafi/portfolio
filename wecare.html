ABOUT THE PROJECT

Design for Personalized Health

During university, I became fascinated with how designers could impact healthcare and biotechnology. My analysis of AI in Medicine and bioinformatics revealed a gap in user-centered design. For my graduation project, I demonstrated the potential of effective biotech products through interdisciplinary collaboration. Mehrdad Moosavi (cancer researcher and computational biologist) offered valuable guidance, highlighting the innovative yet challenging aspects of our work. I was also mentored by Dr. Janessa Laskin (co-lead of the POG program), which significantly enriched the experience. This project stands out as one of the most rewarding of my career, providing crucial lessons in design thinking and collaboration. It was recognized with the ECU Health Design Award for Service, a testament to its impact.

Insights and Problems

It’s hard to make and collect an NFT

Making an NFT right now is hard. You need to know how to set up a crypto wallet, buy ETH, drop it in your wallet, connect your wallet to an NFT website, and then make the NFT. And if creators want their communities to collect NFTs, they have to go through the same arduous process.

There’s no social interaction involved in purchasing an NFT

When you buy an NFT, you may love the creator, but there’s no way to get to know this artist more closely right now.

Many creators are weary of Ethereum’s environmental impact

Ethereum consumes energy at the same rate as the country of Colombia, and many creators and their communities don’t want to participate in NFTs because of it.
